Introduction

Retail Bank's Digital Core Transformation transforms legacy systems into a secure, scalable, cloud-native platform. In this module, you'll explore foundational artifacts—Project Charter, Stakeholder Register, and Engagement Plan—that structure large-scale banking transformation initiatives.
Why you are in this journey

You begin to understand how foundational project artifacts guide a banking transformation. Applying these concepts, you'll learn to connect strategic goals with architecture practices that ensure compliance, alignment, and stakeholder collaboration.
Project charter

The Project Charter authorizes the transformation initiative, defining its scope, governance, and compliance commitments. It transforms Retail Bank's core systems into a real-time, cloud-native architecture, ensuring alignment with KYC, AML, PCI-DSS, and GDPR standards.
Stakeholder register

The Stakeholder Register identifies key contributors from the leadership, compliance, and technology teams. It clarifies roles, influence levels, and communication preferences to foster transparent collaboration and priority management throughout the project lifecycle.
Stakeholder engagement plan

The Stakeholder Engagement Plan maps stakeholder commitment levels and strategies using PMI's engagement model. Structured communication and governance checkpoints focus on maintaining active leadership involvement, regulatory transparency, and end-user inclusion.
Conclusion

Together, these artifacts—the Project Charter, Stakeholder Register, and Stakeholder Engagement Plan—form a cohesive foundation for governance, strategic alignment, and collaborative execution in enterprise-scale digital transformation.
Deliverables

Develop a Project Charter defining objectives, scope, governance, compliance requirements, and strategic intent for Retail Bank's digital transformation.

Build a Stakeholder Register to identify core roles, influence levels, communication preferences, and locations.

Scenario: Digital transformation project for a retail bank using system analysis and data management
Project title

Core digital transformation for retail banking
Date

Current date
Problem or opportunity

A retail bank currently operates legacy systems across critical banking operations, including customer onboarding, transaction processing, and loan servicing. These fragmented systems result in compliance gaps, poor data integrity, and slow service rollouts. A digital core transformation is necessary to modernize the infrastructure, enhance scalability, and improve alignment with compliance.
Current state (As-is)

    Legacy systems with siloed databases and batch processing workflows
    Manual processes, limited real-time data visibility
    High risk of non-compliance with regulatory mandates (KYC, AML, PCI-DSS)

Future state (To-be)

    Unified cloud-native architecture using microservices and containerization
    Real-time data pipelines, centralized data warehouse, secure APIs
    Event-driven alerts for fraud detection and customer activities
    Full auditability, encryption, and role-based access controls

Solution recommendation

Design and implement a scalable, secure, and compliant architecture that integrates the bank's operational systems via APIs, containers, and a cloud data layer. The solution will include CI/CD pipelines, governance frameworks, system blueprints, and role-driven dashboards.

Task 3: Develop a stakeholder engagement plan (PMI scale)

A stakeholder engagement plan helps to identify stakeholders' roles in the retail bank, evaluate their current and preferred commitment levels, and create strategies to shift or maintain commitment levels.
Key steps
Step 1: Download template

Use the Template_Stakeholder Engagement Plan (Word or PDF) and insert your responses in it.

Note: To open the link, Right-click on the it and select Open link in new tab.
Step 2: List stakeholders

In the first column, enter the stakeholder names from the scenario
Step 3: Assess current commitment

Assign a commitment level (U, N, R, S, L) based on their attitude and involvement

Commitment designators

    U (Unaware): The stakeholder is unaware of the project
    N (Neutral): The stakeholder is aware but neither supports nor opposes
    R (Resistant): The stakeholder opposes the project
    S (Supportive): The stakeholder supports the project
    L (Leading): The stakeholder actively drives the project

Step 4: Define the commitment

Assign a commitment level to align with project goals
Step 5: Develop engagement strategies

In the final column, outline strategies to move stakeholders from current to preferred commitment levels or maintain current levels
Step 6: Save your work

Save your stakeholder engagement plan as a PDF and keep it handy for the final project.
